Again - Easy Travel

Roles : UX/UI Designer

Duration : 4 Weeks

Overview

Thorough research is essential for a fantastic trip, enabling a personalised and smooth itinerary. Gathering extensive information, comparing prices and business hours, and organising details can make trip planning feel like a job. This study examines the rapid changes in travel post-pandemic and aims to assist international traveler, whether in small groups (under 4) or solo, in adapting and maximising their trip planning and execution.

Problem

  • Also need to find the best price within a limited budget.
  • Prefer avoiding overcrowded and hot destinations for travel.
  • People Want to fully explore a city with abundant information to read.

Proposed Solutions

  • Generate a smart itinerary based on preferences.
  • Provide a clear Information Architecture
  • Include both hotel and transportation in the package.

"

Solo and small groups of travellers need to have a smart itinerary because planning a hassle-free trip under a limited budget requires spending a large amount of research time.

"

Research

My research initially focused on conducting one-on-one interviews. This approach enabled me to understand the step-by-step process of travel planning and identify specific issues that could cause discomfort. Secondly, I observed the competitive landscape to understand how other itinerary apps are addressing similar concerns.

What I found

  • Users often check multiple websites to compare prices, which is necessary but time-consuming for them.
  • Searching too much takes away the excitement and surprise of discovering a new place.

Reflection

In my first UX search project, I completed the entire A to Z process, which was fulfilling. I realised the significance of pre-work in the design process, from proto-persona to competitor analysis. It serves as a guiding lighthouse when facing confusion during the design progress.

Take Away

1 "Apply the 'Five Whys' technique to gain insights into user behaviour."
The 5 Whys technique is applicable for troubleshooting, quality improvement, and problem-solving, proving most effective for addressing simple or moderately difficult problems.
2 "All effort is worthwhile."
User experience (UX) study is fundamental to your design. The deeper your research, the greater the possibility of solving problems in a human-centred way.
